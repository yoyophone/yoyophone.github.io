1. How to Enable Developer Option#

This guide will help you enable the Developer Option to easily add more apps to your Android Auto device. The first step is to unlock the Developer Option. On your Android device, open the Settings page. Scroll down to the bottom of the page and select “About Phone” or “About Tablet”. Underneath this “About Phone” or “About Tablet” section, find the “Build number”, then tap on it seven times. You will receive a message that says “You are now a developer”, which means you have successfully unlocked the Developer Option.

The second step is to enable the USB debugging. Go back to the Settings page and select “Developer Options”. Underneath “Developer Options”, find “USB Debugging” and enable it. This will help you connect your Android device to your Android Auto device.

The third step is to connect your Android device to your Android Auto device. Connect the USB cable from your Android device to your Android Auto device. You will see “Allow USB Debugging”, select “OK” when it appears on your Android device.

The fourth step is to detect the Android device on your Android Auto device. Open the app and tap the “Connect” button. Tap “Accept” on your Android device and allow permission to use the USB connection for your Android Auto device. You have now successfully enabled Developer Option and added more apps to your Android Auto device.

Q: What is Android Auto?

A: Android Auto is a mobile app developed by Google to mirror features from an Android device, such as Google Maps, to a car’s in-dash information and entertainment head unit. It is available on all Android smartphones.

Q: How do I add more apps to my Android Auto experience? A: To add additional apps to your Android Auto experience, open the Android Auto app on your phone. Tap the “three-dot” icon in the upper-right corner and select “Show all apps.” From there, you can check the boxes next to the apps you’d like to add.

Q: What are some recommended apps for Android Auto? A: Some of the best apps for Android Auto include the Google Play Music, Spotify, Amazon Music, Audible, and NPR One.

Q: How do I know if my phone is compatible with Android Auto? A: Android Auto is available on all Android phones running Android 5.0 (Lollipop) or higher. To check if your phone is compatible, open the Google Play Store app and search for “Android Auto.” If your phone is compatible, you’ll see “Install” in the upper-right corner.

Q: Are there any special settings I need to enable to use Android Auto? A: Yes, in order to use Android Auto, you’ll need to enable a few settings on your phone. Go to Settings > Display > Advanced > Android Auto and make sure the following are toggled on: Auto-launch, Auto-connect to vehicle, Read phone status and identity.
