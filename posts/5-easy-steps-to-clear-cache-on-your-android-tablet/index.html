Do you own an Android Tablet? Is it running slower than usual? If you want to improve the performance of your tablet, try clearing your cache! In this article, we discuss 5 easy steps to clear cache on your Android Tablet. Have you ever experienced a slow running Android tablet? Well, having a sluggish device is a really frustrating experience but the good thing is that it can be fixed easily. All you need to do is to clear the cache on your Android tablet which only takes 5 easy steps!

The first step would be to go to your Settings menu. This is where all of the device’s settings are located. Next, tap on Storage & USB and then on Cached Data. This will remove all of the temporary data that is stored by your apps.

The third step is to go back to the Settings menu and select Apps/Applications Manager. Now go through all of the apps that you are using and select Clear Cache for each of them. This will delete any temporary files that each app has been using.

The fourth step is to go back to the Settings menu, tap on Developer Options, and then select Force Stop on every app. This will effectively close the apps and free up some RAM for your tablet.

Finally, a fifth step is to reboot your Android tablet. This will cause the tablet to clear any remaining caches and remove any RAM that the apps were using.

By following these 5 easy steps, you can quickly clear the cache on your Android tablet and make it run faster. Clearing the cache is something that you should do regularly to ensure that your tablet runs at its optimal performance.
