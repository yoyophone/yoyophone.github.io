When recording sound on your Android phone, one of the most important steps is to choose a mode that will suit your needs. Interview mode is ideal for recording interviews. It enables a voice-activated feature that will start recording only when someone speaks, and it will pause when the speaker pauses. This helps to save a lot of space and also enables you to record conversations with finer detail.

To adjust the sensitivity of the voice-activated feature in interview mode, open the Sound Recorder app, tap the Settings icon, and then select the sensitivity level you need. You can choose between low, medium, or high sensitivity.

1. Activating the Sound Recorder#

Recording audio on your android phone can be tricky, but it’s far from impossible. With the right settings and technique, you can produce a quality recording with ease. One of the main settings you need to consider when using Sound Recorder on your Android phone is the Record Mode. Record Mode determines how the audio is going to be encoded. Choosing the right mode can make a huge difference in terms of audio quality. Three common Record Modes are PCM, AAC and AMR-NB. PCM is generally used for recording speech. AAC is best for medium to high quality audio recording, while AMR-NB is best for lower quality recordings. Depending on the sound you need to record, you should choose the correct mode for best results.

2. Choosing a Record Mode#

When using a sound recorder on your Android phone, you have the option to choose a record mode. Some of the most commonly used record modes are Automatic Gain Control (AGC), Voice Activation (Vox), High Quality (HQ) mode and Normal Quality (NQ) mode. AGC mode is used to automatically adjust the gain to suit the recording environment. Vox mode is used to activate recording only when the sound level reaches a certain threshold. HQ mode is used to provide higher quality recording. NQ mode is used to provide normal quality recording. To choose a record mode, simply open your sound recorder and select the desired option from the list of available modes.
