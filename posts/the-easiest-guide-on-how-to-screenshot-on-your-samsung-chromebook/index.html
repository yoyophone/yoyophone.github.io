1. Overview of How to Screenshot on your Samsung Chromebook#

Grabbing a screenshot of your Samsung Chromebook’s display is a pretty simple and straightforward process. To do this, you will need to press the Ctrl and Switch Window buttons at the same time. The Switch Window button is located in the top row of the keyboard, just above the number row. When you press these two buttons together, a screenshot of your current display will be saved in the Downloads folder in your Files storage. You can also drag and drop the screenshot to the desktop or any other folder of your choice.

In addition, you can also use the Ctrl + Switch Window key combination to take a portion of the current display. This is done by pressing and holding the two buttons together and then dragging the cursor across the desired area. You can then release the keys to take the screenshot and it will be saved in the same Downloads folder as above.

You can also press the Ctrl + Shift + Refresh buttons at the same time to take a full page screenshot of your current display. Once the screenshot has been captured, it will be saved as a .png file in the same Downloads folder.

Furthermore, you can press the Ctrl + Shift + F5 keys to take a delayed screenshot. This allows you to open menus, windows, and other components to take a full-page screenshot of your display when it is ready. The screenshot will be saved as a .png file in the same Downloads folder.

That’s it! You now know how to take a screenshot of your Samsung Chromebook’s display. Give it a try and learn how to capture the images you need with ease.
